# Target Customer Research: Real-Time Collaboration

## Customer Segments

### Enterprise Teams (Primary Target)
**Profile:** 50+ employees, distributed teams, high collaboration needs
**Current TaskFlow Usage:** Power users, average 40+ hours/week
**Annual Contract Value:** $25K - $100K+

**Key Pain Points:**
- "We waste 2-3 hours per week resolving version conflicts" - TechCorp PM
- "Our distributed team across 3 time zones needs to see updates instantly" - Global Enterprises
- "Real-time collaboration is non-negotiable for our renewal" - InnovateCo (at-risk account)

**Expected Impact:**
- Reduces onboarding friction for new team members
- Enables synchronous planning sessions
- Critical for complex, multi-stakeholder projects

---

### SMB Teams (Secondary Target)
**Profile:** 10-50 employees, growing fast, tech-forward
**Current TaskFlow Usage:** Regular users, average 20 hours/week
**Annual Contract Value:** $5K - $15K

**Key Pain Points:**
- "We love TaskFlow but miss the real-time feel of Google Docs" - StartupXYZ
- "Hard to coordinate when everyone's working remote" - Agency Teams
- "Want to see what my team is working on without asking" - Small Business Owner

**Expected Impact:**
- Increases engagement and daily active usage
- Reduces need for status meetings
- Makes TaskFlow feel more modern and responsive

---

## Customer Feedback Highlights

### From User Interviews (January 2025)

**Enterprise Segment (8 interviews):**
- 7 out of 8 cited real-time collaboration as "critical" or "very important"
- 5 mentioned competitors (Asana, Linear) have this feature
- 3 are currently evaluating alternatives specifically for this capability
- Average willingness to pay: +15% premium for real-time features

**Top Quotes:**
> "When three PMs are planning a sprint, we need to see each other's changes live. Right now we use a Google Doc and copy-paste into TaskFlow later."
> — Sarah Chen, PM Lead at TechCorp

> "Our CEO asked why we're paying $75K/year for a tool that doesn't show real-time updates. I couldn't give a good answer."
> — Michael Rodriguez, Head of Product at InnovateCo

> "The refresh button is my least favorite feature in TaskFlow."
> — Jamie Patel, Engineering Manager at DistributedTeams Inc

**SMB Segment (12 interviews):**
- 9 out of 12 said real-time updates would increase daily usage
- 6 mentioned they use Slack/Discord to coordinate because TaskFlow feels "static"
- All agreed it would reduce confusion and version conflicts

**Top Quotes:**
> "We're a remote-first company. Real-time collaboration isn't a nice-to-have, it's how we work."
> — Alex Kim, Founder at RemoteAgency

> "I want to see my team's activity like I see cursors in Figma. Makes collaboration feel alive."
> — Taylor Morrison, Design Lead at CreativeStudio

## Segment Prioritization Recommendation

**Phase 1 (Beta - Week 1-2):**
- Launch to Enterprise customers first
- Focus on at-risk accounts (InnovateCo, TechCorp, Global Enterprises)
- High-touch support and feedback collection

**Phase 2 (General Availability - Week 3-4):**
- Roll out to all Enterprise customers
- Begin SMB rollout with self-serve activation
- Monitor performance and scale infrastructure

**Rationale:**
- Enterprise customers have immediate renewal pressure
- Higher ACV makes them priority for retention
- Beta feedback from power users will improve SMB experience
- Enterprise success stories will drive SMB adoption
