# Proposal: Q3 Company Offsite — Portland, OR

## Executive Summary
Employee engagement scores dropped 18% year-over-year, and cross-team collaboration ratings are at an all-time low. A 3-day company offsite in Portland, OR (September 17-19, 2026) would bring all 85 employees together for strategic alignment, team-building, and culture investment. Estimated cost: $74,000 ($870/person). Expected impact: measurable improvement in engagement scores and a shared roadmap entering Q4.

## Problem
We've been fully remote since 2022, and it's showing. The last engagement survey (February 2026) flagged three areas in the red:
- **Cross-department collaboration:** 42% favorable (down from 67% in 2024)
- **Connection to company mission:** 51% favorable (down from 74%)
- **"I have a friend at work":** 38% favorable (down from 61%)

Exit interviews from the last 6 months cite "feeling disconnected" as the #2 reason for leaving, behind only compensation. We've lost 11 people this year — at an estimated $45K replacement cost each, that's roughly $500K in turnover costs partially attributable to culture erosion.

## Proposed Solution
A 3-day offsite in Portland, OR combining strategic working sessions with structured team-building. Portland offers direct flights from our three largest employee hubs (SF, Seattle, Denver), keeps costs reasonable, and gives us access to unique team experiences.

Deliverables:
- Full event plan with agenda, logistics, and vendor contracts
- Pre-offsite survey to identify topics employees most want to address
- Three strategic working sessions (company roadmap, cross-team projects, operating norms)
- Two structured team-building activities (not trust falls — actual experiences)
- Post-offsite action plan with owners and deadlines
- 30-day follow-up survey to measure impact

## Scope
**Included:** Flights and ground transport for 85 employees, 2 nights hotel, all meals, venue rental for working sessions, two team activities, event planning and coordination, swag bags.
**Not included:** Significant others/families, pre- or post-offsite vacation extensions (employees can book on their own), international travel for 3 contractors based overseas (they'll join via video for key sessions).

## Timeline
- **April 1-15:** Venue research and RFP to 3 event planning vendors
- **April 16-30:** Select vendor, sign contracts, book hotel room block
- **May 1-31:** Design agenda, book team activities, send pre-offsite survey
- **June 1-30:** Finalize agenda based on survey results, book flights
- **July 1 - Aug 31:** Logistics coordination, swag production, prep materials
- **September 17-19:** Offsite event
- **October 17:** Post-offsite survey and impact report

## Resources Needed
- 1 internal project lead (Operations Manager, ~20% time for 5 months)
- Event planning vendor ($12,000 estimated)
- Hotel room block: 85 rooms × 2 nights × $189/night = $32,130
- Flights: 85 employees × avg $280 round-trip = $23,800
- Meals and catering: $3,200 (group dinners and working lunches)
- Venue rental for sessions: $1,800/day × 2 days = $3,600
- Team activities: $2,400 (guided group experiences)
- Swag and materials: $1,500
- Contingency (10%): $7,400
- **Total estimated budget: $74,030**

## Success Metrics
- Cross-department collaboration score increases from 42% to 55%+ on next engagement survey
- "Connection to company mission" increases from 51% to 65%+
- At least 3 cross-team initiatives launched within 30 days of offsite
- 80%+ of attendees rate the offsite as "valuable" or "very valuable" in post-event survey
- Voluntary attrition rate decreases from current 14% annualized to below 10% by end of Q4

## Risks and Mitigations
- **Budget overrun:** 10% contingency buffer built in. Venue and hotel contracts will include fixed pricing. Flight costs locked by booking 90 days out.
- **Low attendance:** Frame as mandatory with 4+ months notice. Accommodate edge cases (parental leave, medical) with recorded sessions and a follow-up 1:1 with their manager.
- **"Just another offsite" cynicism:** Pre-survey ensures agenda reflects what employees actually care about. Working sessions produce real deliverables, not just motivational talks.
- **Travel disruptions:** Portland has 3 major airports within range. Build in flexible arrival windows — Day 1 starts at 1 PM, not 9 AM.
